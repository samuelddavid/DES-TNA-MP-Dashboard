Integrated Prioritisation Matrix — Combined Score (Demand × Gap)

Combined Score = (Priority Signal % + Need Signal %) ÷ 2  ·  Source: Indore Pilot TNA Report, April 2026 (STAT Framework, UNSD/GIST)  ·  Scores ≥50 = Immediate; 35–49 = Phase 1B; <35 = Reinforcement

How to read this matrix (methodology: Indore Pilot TNA Report, Table 6.1–6.2) Priority Signal (%) — The percentage of respondents who selected at least one competency in this theme as a top support need. Measures expressed demand — what staff say they need.
Need Signal (%) — The weighted skill gap: (4 − average score) ÷ 4 × 100, weighted by number of respondents per post group (BLI, ASO, DSO, etc.). Measures actual capability distance from expert level independent of stated demand.
Combined Score — Simple average of both signals (50:50). Balances what staff ask for with what their self-assessed scores reveal. A high combined score means both high demand AND a large gap — the strongest signal for intervention.
Immediate (Phase 1A) Score ≥ 50: Highest urgency. Design and deliver in first training cycle.
Phase 1B Score 35–49: Important. Schedule in first cycle, after Immediate themes.
Reinforcement Score <35: Lower urgency. Embed as supplementary modules in later cycles.
The seven themes follow exactly Table 6.1 of the Indore Division Pilot TNA Report, Pahlé India Foundation (PIF), New Delhi, April 2026. Thresholds follow Table 6.2 of the same report.
